# Marquee GTM: The Solo-Operator Operating System

This is Sarah's complete go-to-market model for Marquee, run by one person with AI doing the work of a team. It is deliberately different from generic startup GTM. Generic playbooks are built for sales-led B2B SaaS: cold outreach, SDRs, demo calls, pipelines, high ACV. **Marquee is B2C.** It sells a $46-a-year product to individual professionals who buy it themselves. The motion is consumer and creator-led, not sales-led. Apply an outbound, pipeline motion here and you waste months.

The core belief: **the brand is the marketing.** You build an audience by creating, curating, and connecting, then launch products into that audience the way a creator launches a product line. PR, riding coattails, owning the data, and creative are the amplifiers. The funnel falls out of the brand, it is never bolted on.

Sarah reacts to concrete builds, not abstract questions. Default to showing real options over asking.

## This is a sequence of dominoes, not a pile of tactics

The single most important thing in this skill: **these are dominoes, and they fall in order.** Each one has to knock over the next. You do not amplify before you have validated. You do not scale a format before you have found one. You do not turn on partnerships before you have a result worth co-branding. Run them out of order and you spend money and months on dominoes that never tip.

**`references/the-sequence.md` is the master file. Read it first for any "what do I do, in what order, who do I have to be, what does it cost" question.** It lays out the full critical path domino by domino, and for each one: what knocks it over, what it unlocks, the roles Sarah plays in it, the AI leverage, and the cost. It also holds the prioritization rule for when everything is screaming at once.

The capability areas, each a reference file the sequence draws on:

- **Creator engine** — live the pain, drop hints, video, authentic connection, IRL and swag, the target-interview-feature-tag creator play. → `references/creator-engine.md`
- **Channels** — Instagram first, TikTok, Reddit seeding, SEO, GEO, email. No LinkedIn yet. → `references/channels.md`
- **Partnerships (one-to-many)** — community partnerships like Rhize Together with thousands of members, co-branded Blueprints, creator partnerships. One partner, many members. → `references/partnerships.md`
- **Data and PR** — own the data with your own surveys, the author PR blitz, interviews. Be the source others cite. → `references/data-and-pr.md`
- **Revenue engine** — the self-serve sales motion (there is no salesperson, but there is absolutely a sales function), conversion, customer service, retention. → `references/revenue-engine.md`
- **Systems and stack** — ManyChat, email, CRM, funnels, automation. Nothing leaks. → `references/systems-and-stack.md`
- **Beta and pricing** — paid beta, feedback loops, testing pricing and messaging before scaling. → `references/beta-and-pricing.md`
- **Budget** — the real monthly cost of the stack, what each phase costs to run, where the first dollars go. → `references/budget.md`
- **Milestones and roles** — revenue milestones unlock the next hire, plus the honest map of doing every function alone. → `references/milestones-and-roles.md`

The chain recycles at the top once it is running: members and takers become the data and the community that feed the next round. But it only recycles after it has fallen all the way through once. Read the relevant reference file before building in that area, and place every piece of work on the domino it belongs to.

## The living knowledge base (this is how the skill learns)

The skill has two layers. The **strategy layer** is the spine and the `references/` files: the playbook, which changes slowly and on purpose. The **knowledge layer** is `knowledge/`, built to grow constantly. This separation is deliberate, so that learning the market never risks breaking the strategy, and so that updating the strategy is a considered act, not an accident.

The knowledge files:

- `knowledge/competitors.md` — who else is in the space, their gaps, dated notes as they move.
- `knowledge/market.md` — every stat the brand cites with its source, plus our own survey data as it accrues. The data half of the voice only works if it is real.
- `knowledge/feedback.md` — append-only log of what real people say (beta, quiz, comments, churn, creators). The single most valuable input to iteration.
- `knowledge/playbook-library.md` — a swipe file of other GTM models, what to steal and what to reject. New playbooks get logged here, filtered through the non-negotiables.
- `knowledge/decisions.md` — the log of what was decided, why, and what changed. The memory that keeps settled questions from drifting back open and makes iteration traceable.

**The protocol, every session:**

1. **Read before acting.** Before positioning or messaging, read `competitors.md` and `decisions.md`. Before citing a number, read `market.md`. Before changing the result, pricing, or product, read `feedback.md`. Before designing a tactic, check `playbook-library.md`.
2. **Write after learning.** When you learn something new, capture it in the right file, dated, append rather than overwrite. A competitor moves, log it. Feedback comes in, log it verbatim. A new playbook is read, log what to steal and reject. A decision is made or reversed, log it with the reason.
3. **Never let an unsourced number or a killed idea resurface.** `market.md` gates the stats. `decisions.md` gates the killed ideas. Check them.

Because the whole skill lives in the GitHub repo, every edit is versioned. The history of `decisions.md` and the growth of `feedback.md` and `market.md` are the record of how the strategy evolved. To iterate the strategy itself, edit the `references/` files deliberately and log the change in `decisions.md`. To grow the knowledge, append to `knowledge/` freely. Keep those two motions distinct.

## You play every role at once, so prioritize by the earliest unfallen domino

At every step Sarah is the brand, the writer, the editor, the community manager, the partnerships lead, the data and PR shop, the customer service desk, and the operator, all at the same time. That is not a failure to fix, it is the job, and AI is what makes it survivable. The way to not drown: **work the critical role of the earliest domino that has not fallen yet, and let everything else run on automation, templates, or maintenance.** The single highest-leverage thing in any given week is the bottleneck on the current domino. Everything downstream of it is a distraction until it tips. The role-load and the priority call for each domino are spelled out in `references/the-sequence.md`.

## Non-negotiables (these keep getting lost, so hold them hard)

- **B2C.** We sell to individuals, with a consumer marketing motion. Not B2B, not sales-led.
- **v1 is the candidate side only.** No company login, search, or discovery. Employer accounts, matching, hiring tools, and the company pipeline are v2. If a line implies a company is doing something on the platform, it is v2. Do not let it drift into v1.
- **The brand is the marketing.** Build audience by creating, curating, connecting. Launch into it. Do not lead with the product.
- **Trust first, product last.** Earn a point of view on the world before asking anyone to do anything.
- **Self-serve.** No demos, no sales calls, no pipeline, no "book a demo." The product sells itself because every friction point is removed.
- **Build with creators, never cold-barter them.** Co-create, feature, tag, run series with them. A handful of real relationships, not fifty cold DMs and an affiliate link.
- **Build the plane while flying it.** A few meaningful referral structures defined when you reach them, not a coefficient machine planned up front.
- **No invented mechanics or growth gimmicks.** There is no "card loop." No "resume funeral." No empty growth-hack language like "value-first seeding." If a tactic sounds like a growth hack, cut it.
- **WFH (the media property) is real but currently deprioritized.** Do not make it central or assume it runs. Until Sarah revives it, the brand creates and curates as Marquee.
- **ELVISS is structure, not decoration.** Experience, Leadership, Values, Impact, Skills, Story are navigation, never visible letter badges.

## Voice (for any copy, post, or message)

Punchy, human, storytelling first, culturally fluent. **Humor and data in equal measure, and always real.** Never corporate, never try-hard. No em dashes in body copy. Numerals, not spelled-out percentages (75%, not seventy-five percent). No AI-sounding choppy sentences used as rhetorical punches. No invented section headers. Ground every claim in real data (ghosting rates, ATS filter rates, fractional growth, the 6-second resume scan), never fabricated social proof.

## The funnel (it falls out of the brand)

1. Content builds the audience and earns the point of view.
2. The point of view names the pain people already feel (filtered, ghosted, replaceable, invisible) so they feel seen.
3. The quiz, the Alignment Blueprint, is the first thing they actually do, not just read, because by the time you ask it answers a question they are already sitting in.
4. The result hands them their edge and opens a gap: "okay, so where do I actually show this."
5. Marquee closes the gap. The result ends on the one thing they cannot do for free yet, turn this into a living page real people can see.
6. PR, coattails, and owned data widen the top of all of it.

Because it is self-serve, remove every friction point in steps 3 to 5. Nobody should need to talk to anyone to take the quiz, see the result, or become a member. The systems in stage 4 are what make this real.

## Taste as moat: what only Sarah does vs what AI does

The moat is judgment, not headcount. The rule: **if it repeats, automate it; if it needs judgment, template it.** Sarah owns the point of view, the voice of every post, which moments to ride, creator and partner relationships, pricing, and what to say no to. AI does throughput: drafting in her voice, turning one idea into ten formats, mining Reddit and communities for the real language of the pain, pulling the data story, scheduling, repurposing, first-line customer service. The full function-by-function split is in `references/milestones-and-roles.md`.

On sales specifically: there is no salesperson and never will be at this motion, but there is absolutely a sales function. It is the self-serve conversion engine, the result-to-paid moment, the lifecycle nurture, the offer and the price. Treat it as real revenue work, not an afterthought. It lives in `references/revenue-engine.md`. Customer service is part of the same function, because at $46/yr renewals, service is retention is revenue.

## The phase framework (the part that is an actual framework)

Every phase has one goal, a time split, a do-list, an explicit not-yet list, and a hard trigger to move on. What works in one phase hurts in the next. Milestones are paying members, not B2B MRR. Roughly: ~200 members is the first real mark, ~2,000 the second, ~10,000 the third.

**Phase 1 — Validation. First ~100 members.**
Goal: prove the Blueprint result is good enough that people share it unprompted and a first handful pay. Signal, not scale.
Time: 50% building audience and point of view, 30% getting the quiz in front of real people in motion and watching them react, 20% polishing the result and the free-to-paid moment.
Do: post daily in Sarah's voice, get the quiz in front of a small real group, validate the result with about 10 target people, charge from day one, run the paid beta (`references/beta-and-pricing.md`).
Not yet: PR blitz, paid creators, partnerships, the big data report.
Move on when: people share their result unprompted and the first cohort converts at a rate worth multiplying. If they do not share it, stay here. Do not automate a weak result.

**Phase 2 — Traction. ~200 to 2,000 members.**
Goal: find the one repeatable content format that reliably turns audience into takers into members.
Time: 55% distribution and content, 25% community and the create-curate-connect rhythm, 20% product.
Do: test two or three formats and hooks with a real run each, turn on PR and coattails as point-of-view-led, start the handful of real creator collaborations, run your first surveys so you start owning data, write down which hooks convert.
Not yet: scaling anything, hiring beyond the first contractor, paid ads.
Move on when: one format reliably produces takers and free-to-paid holds. One channel proven before a second.

**Phase 3 — Scale the machine. ~2,000 to 10,000 members.**
Goal: systematize the winning format with AI and multiply output without losing Sarah's voice.
Time: 30% systems and AI production, 30% distribution, 25% product, 15% strategy.
Do: repurpose the winning format across channels at volume while Sarah keeps taste and voice, ship the owned-data PR story, run the author PR blitz, expand creators, revisit annual pricing. AI scales production here, not before.
Not yet: a full team.
Move on when: the machine runs at volume and revenue per Sarah-hour is climbing.

**Phase 4 — Leverage. 10,000+ members.**
Goal: most outcome per founder-hour, decide where one fractional person removes the biggest bottleneck.
Do: hold every hour to a real value bar, bring fractional help on the single biggest constraint, build the moats that compound (the data, the community, the depth of the profile). Fractional first.

The teeth are the triggers, not the activities. Phase 1 stays manual on purpose. Phase 2 proves one channel before a second. Phase 3 is where AI scales production. Do not scale before the result and the format are proven.

## The GTM elements (the complete backbone, kept in one place)

Every go-to-market has the same core elements. This is the canonical list, answered for Marquee, with a pointer to where each one is built out. The dominoes are the order things happen in. These elements are the what. Keep them sharp and current, because a fuzzy element anywhere shows up as a leak somewhere downstream.

1. **Target market and segments.** Primary wedge is one person, the **person in motion** (laid off, pivoting, going fractional, or quietly stuck and starting to look). Secondary segments to expand into later: fractional and portfolio professionals, operators in transition, senior ICs and strategists, founders re-entering, new graduates, the passively present. Name the primary, speak to it first, do not dilute it. Confirm or re-cut the wedge with Sarah but commit to one.
2. **Buyer persona.** Who they are, where they already spend attention (Instagram, TikTok, Reddit, not LinkedIn yet), the pain in their own words (filtered, ghosted, replaceable, invisible), and why they will want this right now. Build it from real language mined from communities, not assumptions.
3. **Positioning and value proposition.** The one-sentence value prop, the shortest and hardest-won line in the plan. Working positioning: for professionals done fighting the algorithm and done paying for a site they will never update, Marquee turns your career into a living, shareable page you own, built in minutes, true to your voice.
4. **Brand messaging.** The messaging pillars everything ladders to, the tone, and the primary line. Working primary line: "Be known, not filtered." Voice rules are in the spine. Humor and data in equal measure, always real.
5. **Pricing model.** $46/yr, framed honestly as one coffee a month, a value statement that breaks the SaaS pricing reflex. Free layer (quiz, archetype, dashboard, shareable result) versus gated Marquee membership. Pricing and framing are levers to test, not settings. See `references/beta-and-pricing.md`.
6. **Sales strategy and channels.** Self-serve. No salesperson, but a real sales function: the result-to-paid moment, the nurture, the offer. Full detail in `references/revenue-engine.md`.
7. **Marketing strategy and channels.** Creator-led and brand-first. Instagram primary, TikTok second, Reddit seeded, SEO and GEO compounding, email owned, no LinkedIn yet, paid parked. One-to-many partnerships as the leverage move. See `references/channels.md`, `references/creator-engine.md`, `references/partnerships.md`, `references/data-and-pr.md`.
8. **Budget and resource allocation.** Kept lean and funded by revenue, scaling by phase. Full monthly numbers in `references/budget.md`.
9. **Performance indicators.** The few numbers that actually decide things (next section). Ignore vanity metrics.

## Performance indicators (the numbers that matter)

- **Do people share the result unprompted?** The single Domino 0 and Phase 1 signal. If not, fix the result before anything else.
- **Free to paid.** The conversion the whole model rests on. Watch it from the first cohort.
- **One repeatable format that converts.** The Domino 4 unlock. Until one exists, do not scale.
- **Renewal rate.** The hidden one. Annual billing masks churn for a year, and the milestone math assumes members stay. See `references/revenue-engine.md`.

## Mistakes to refuse

- Selling to everyone. One wedge or it lands with no one.
- A fuzzy value proposition. If the one-sentence prop is not sharp, distribution will not save it.
- Ignoring early reaction. The first takers tell you for free whether the result lands.
- Inconsistent messaging across channels. One voice everywhere.
- Scaling before it is proven. Find the result and the format first, then scale with AI.
- Building for Sarah's excitement instead of the wedge's pull.
- Reaching for sales-led tactics because a generic framework suggested them. Wrong motion. Redirect to brand-first, B2C.

## How to show up in this work

Lead with concrete options Sarah can react to. Keep copy in her voice and the rules above. Place every tactic in the loop, tie it to a phase and to one of the three numbers, or cut it. When a generic GTM idea appears, check it against the non-negotiables before it ever reaches her. Read the relevant reference file before building in a given stage.
